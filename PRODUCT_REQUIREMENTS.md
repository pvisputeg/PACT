# PACT product requirements - Operation Northstar

## Product statement

PACT is an Enterprise Outcome Operating System that turns a consequential business signal into proof, impact, an independently challenged decision, human authorization, coordinated action, observation, closeout, and learning.

## Flagship outcome

Protect at least 95% of $8.7M in committed NX-400 revenue over 21 days, spend no more than $420,000, use only approved or conditionally approved suppliers, preserve every quality threshold, and prevent unauthorized customer communication.

## Required journey

1. Mission Control ranks Strategic Material Recovery first.
2. Northstar Plant 7 Digital Twin exposes machines, people, inventory, orders, suppliers, logistics, quality, finance, and outcome risk.
3. Investigation treats the 12-day Meridian Horizon diversion as potentially material and verification-required.
4. A named human accepts the Outcome Contract before planning.
5. Proofline uses ten controls to reconcile 8.1 ERP days to 5.4 usable days.
6. Impact traces Copper Alloy C-17 through the heat-exchanger cell, Line C, 318 orders, 42 customers, and $8.7M exposure.
7. Speed First fails budget; Cost First fails target; Balanced Recovery projects 96.4% for $386,000.
8. A separate Auditor challenges quality timing, customs, labor, compressor risk, and contingency.
9. The Plant COO approves a bounded Action Contract with persistent conditions.
10. Supplier commitment before quality approval fails with the exact required error.
11. Ten actions across eight functions execute only when predecessors and authority pass.
12. The twin progresses from 5.4 to 7.1, 8.7, and 9.8 coverage days.
13. Measurement distinguishes target 95.0%, simulation 96.4%, and observed synthetic 96.1%.
14. Closeout records $389,000 final spend, zero lost strategic customers, zero quality incidents, and zero unauthorized sends.
15. Ledger and replay preserve the full chain and eight learning records.

## AI boundary

- Outcome Lead: synthesize, compare, explain, and propose strict structured output.
- Independent Auditor: challenge a sealed packet and recommend conditions.
- Human approver: authorize the Action Contract.
- Deterministic policy: validate and execute local synthetic tools.

Models may not approve spend, execute tools, bypass constraints, send communication, or describe a simulation as observed.

## Evidence requirements

Every material conclusion must cite scenario evidence and use one of: `FACT`, `CALCULATED`, `INFERRED`, `ESTIMATED`, `SIMULATED`, or `OBSERVED`. Projections and associations never become observations or exclusive causal claims.

## Safety requirements

- exact plan and audit condition IDs;
- named human approval;
- maximum $420,000 authority;
- approved supplier scope;
- quality release before supplier commitment;
- finance authorization before supplier commitment;
- customs contingency before transfer;
- material and labor readiness before resequencing;
- direct customer sending unavailable;
- all outcomes written to the ledger.

## Non-functional requirements

- fixed seed 56021;
- synthetic local environment only;
- responsive dark executive interface with reduced-motion support;
- all derived headline numbers reproducible from data and domain functions;
- reset returns outcome, approvals, actions, twin, ledger, observations, and replay to clean state;
- sub-three-minute judge path;
- no deploy or publish action in the implementation workflow.

## Acceptance commands

`npm install`, `npm run reset`, `npm test`, `npm run verify:mcp`, `npm run judge:verify`, `npm run build`, `npm run verify:dist`, `npm run validate:plugin`, and `npm run release:audit`.
